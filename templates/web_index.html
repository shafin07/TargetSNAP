📖 TargetSNAP — User Guide

1. Overview

TargetSNAP analyses how a single-nucleotide polymorphism (SNP) in a gene’s 3′ UTR affects miRNA binding. It runs the TargetScan 7 pipeline locally on both the reference and alternate allele sequences, then compares the predicted miRNA target sites to identify gains or losses of miRNA regulation. Each prediction is further validated by RNAhybrid minimum free energy (MFE) calculation.

2. Workflow

  • Search — Enter an rs ID. The tool maps it to overlapping genes and retrieves their 3′ UTR transcript sequences.
  • Annotations — ClinVar clinical significance, GWAS Catalog trait associations, and phastCons46way conservation score are fetched automatically for the SNP.
  • Select Transcript — Choose a transcript isoform. The tool substitutes the REF and ALT alleles into the UTR.
  • TargetScan Pipeline — Seed matching (targetscan_70.pl) identifies miRNA binding sites; context++ scoring (targetscan_70_context_scores.pl) calculates binding strength.
  • RNAhybrid MFE — For each miRNA, RNAhybrid computes the minimum free energy of the miRNA:UTR duplex for both REF and ALT alleles, providing a thermodynamic validation of binding.
  • Comparison — REF and ALT predictions are compared to classify each miRNA as LOF, GOF, or Neutral.

3. Context++ Score

TargetScan produces a context++ score for each predicted miRNA–target interaction. A more negative raw score indicates stronger repression. Internally, TargetSNAP negates this value so that a higher displayed score means stronger predicted binding.

Raw Context++ ScoreRepression Level
≤ −0.3Strong
−0.3 to −0.15Moderate
−0.15 to 0Weak
≥ 0None

4. RNAhybrid MFE & P-value

RNAhybrid predicts the energetically most favourable hybridisation between the miRNA and the UTR region. It is complementary to TargetScan’s seed-based approach because it considers full-length thermodynamic stability.

MetricMeaning
MFE (kcal/mol)Minimum free energy of the most stable duplex. More negative = stronger binding. Displayed as REF / ALT.
p-valueProbability of observing an equally good or better hit by chance in the 3′ UTR background distribution (-s 3utr_human). Lower = more significant.
ΔMFEALT MFE minus REF MFE. Positive = weaker ALT binding (supports LOF). Negative = stronger ALT binding (supports GOF).

7. SNP Annotations

  • ClinVar — Clinical significance from NCBI ClinVar (pathogenic / benign / VUS). Queried via eUtils API.
  • GWAS Catalog — Genome-wide association study traits linked to the SNP via the EBI GWAS REST API. Shows p-values and risk alleles.
  • Conservation — PhastCons 46-way vertebrate conservation at the SNP position (UCSC REST). Score 0–1; higher = more conserved across species. Highly conserved SNP positions are more likely to be functionally important.

8. Tissue Expression Filter

Use the tissue dropdown to highlight miRNAs known to be highly expressed in a specific tissue/organ. Curated from TCGA and miRmine expression databases. miRNAs enriched in the selected tissue are marked with a Tissue ✓ badge and sorted first; non-relevant miRNAs are dimmed.

10. Exporting Results

Click the download buttons (CSV, TSV, or JSON) above the results to export all LOF, GOF, and neutral miRNA records. The export includes all columns: context++ scores, RNAhybrid MFE, p-values, ΔMFE, priority scores, site types, and binding change annotations.

11. Tips

  • Large UTRs (> 3000 bp) may take several minutes on first run. Results are cached afterward.
  • Use Top 10 / Show All to quickly see the most impactful miRNAs vs. the full list.
  • Use the “Show only changed bindings” toggle to filter to miRNAs with allelic differences.
  • Neutral section is capped at 100 entries — total count is shown in the header.
  • ClinVar/GWAS lookups run asynchronously and won’t slow down the main analysis.
  • RNAhybrid runs in parallel for all miRNAs during the pipeline — no extra wait.
